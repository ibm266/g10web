### **African American Traditions**

African American wedding customs are filled with love, rich history, and timeless traditions. 

  * The _Jumping of the Broom_ depicts a couple entering together a new chapter of their lives or the ethos of embarking on a fresh start, practising this tradition rooted in slavery. 

  * The Libation Ceremony, where a liquid is poured to bless the ancestors, also marks the Primordial essence. These customs combine past and present to ensure a united culture, and their family and legacy live on. Each movement depicts a story and defends community culture.




### **Cambodian Ceremonies**

Cambodian weddings tend to celebrate the Gaat Sah, a ritual that is performed annually on the wedding anniversary of the couple and features belly dancers as its star performers. During the ceremony of this ritual, belly dancers are accompanied by the spouses in a joyful celebration who are anointed as friends. The husbands of the earliest performers transcend to second wives, which augurs as a sign of fertility as the couple prepares to start a new life together; hence, the process signifies renewal and purification. Spinning around a fire, the folks circumambulate, awaiting their names to be called in a symbolic fashion and on a path to initiation into adulthood.

### **Korean Wedding Traditions**

The Paebaek is a symbolic and unique ceremony from Korea rich in heritage that celebrates unity during betrothal. As part of this solid activity, the female participant must always wear beautiful colourful gowns which are sought after during this joyful occasion. In honouring the groom's family and paying dual respect to the couple, the participants in this festivity honourably kneel and emit joyous foot tapping as the date and another spouse signifies wives of childbirth to come forth with more life. The tempered climate consists of enlightened eyeballs alongside more naive lively chatter. Painstaking effort is intertwined to bind thus strongly the Paebaek which incorporates their culture with yet unexplained but ingrained and ungrammatical English words in the pattern of the union.

### **Eastern Orthodox Practices**

The spectacular element of Eastern Orthodox weddings is the Crowning Ceremony, which radiates spiritual beauty. During this ceremony, prayers joyously surround the couple, and a crown is placed on their heads, signifying the glory and honour of the new bond. The couple also receives the divine mark of blessing, and they share endorsement as partners ruling the household. The moment is filled with sacredness and joy and reminds everyone of the essence of the wedding as an amalgamation of love, faith, and commitment. 

### **Indian Wedding Traditions**

The heart of Indian weddings is the Saptapadi ceremony, which is brimming with wonderful spiritual meaning. Flickers of flames surround the couple and hold sacred in front of the family as they promise to try to give respect, love, and companionship in return for the seven steps they commit to making. Each step captures the essence of commitment and sacred partnership, adding an abundant bond in devotion and shared purpose.

### **Filipino Traditions**

The Cord-and-Veil **cultural wedding customs​** are an exquisite wedding custom showing two people's union and marital commitment. The veil encircles the couple and symbolizes their lives woven together under the veil of love. A cord is placed over the couple's shoulders in an infinity loop, signifying their everlasting bond.

### **Japanese Traditions**

The San-san-kudo ritual in Japanese weddings encapsulates unity and harmony. This ritual showcases the elegant bride and groom tips with “three-time” from three sake cups and celebrates the joining of two souls and families. Three hold strong spiritual significance, representing the past, present, and future. Each sip deepens their mutual respect and love. This timeless ceremony, steeped in grace, exemplifies the essence of togetherness and the hopeful beginning of a partnered lifetime.

### **Tea Ceremony in Chinese Weddings**

The Chinese tea ceremony is one of the most beautiful ceremonies you will see. The love of family and warmth surrounds it and emerges as a “true tribute” tradition that honors respect and unity. In “elegant dresses,” the bride and groom pour the tea for their elders into delicate, sophisticated porcelain cups. Whenever any elder member takes a sip, it symbolizes “gratitude, respect, and unification” for both families. 

### **Lasso Ceremony (Latin America)**

Lasso Ceremony is one of the most stunning traditions that simultaneously symbolize oneness and infinity during a wedding in Latin America. It's the most creative tradition you will ever witness because the “bordered figure of eight” is made with a lasso on the couple’s shoulders. This act indicates couples' undying love and commitment towards each other. With every prayer uttered by the officiant, the lasso ceases to be an adornment but an embodiment of the love for the couple's journey and their step towards togetherness. 

### **Zaffe (Lebanon)**

This lively procession, consisting of musicians, drummers, and dancers to celebrate the couple's joining, turns Lebanese weddings into a celebration of zeal and enthusiasm we call Zaffe. Accompanied by clapping and traditional dabke dancing, Zaffe is a celebration filled with sound that unites friends and relatives. Musicians lead the charge, announcing the couple's union in thrilling detail, while the bride and groom become the centre of this spirited celebration, showered in joy and music as they embark on their new lives.

### **Bread and Salt Blessing (Poland)**

In Polish weddings, the evocation of family and society is honoured with the Bread and Salt Blessing. The ceremony is marked by parents gifting the couple a loaf of bread, which evokes wealth and salt, symbolizing hardship. The parents aim to grant these tokens wishing for durability and abundance in marriage, which acknowledges the highs and lows of life. It combines practicality with sentimental value, reinforcing the bonds of joy and hardship interlacing several generations on the wedding day.

### **Culture Valued in Weddings through Music and Dance**

Life at weddings is personified through music and dance as it focuses on transforming the ceremony into a celebration of love, unity, and culture. Every step and rhythm of the dances is significant as it promotes joy and connections among people. 

Here are some beautiful examples of how different cultural dances shape these special moments:

#### **Nigerian Money Dance**

Envision a wedding event where prosperity rains from the sky! This celebratory Money dance tradition transforms the floor into a land of plenty, where everyone is thankful and wishes for the couple’s prosperous future and the whole community.

#### **Caribbean Palance Dance**

The palance dance is among the most electrifying and high-octane dances usually performed during the traditional Caribbean weddings​. The participants ' unified rhythms and synchronized steps lead to a unique display of togetherness and celebration. With every beat and step, joy is expressed in its rawest form to show the happiness of the bride and groom, their family, and their friends.

#### **Cuban Conga Line**

Very few traditions epitomize togetherness like the Cuban Conga Line. This lively spiral formation of guests moving in a straight line and dancing around the wedding venue brings fun and laughter to the event. Everyone links arms, which signifies the couple’s new family and the community as they embark on this new adventure.

#### **Irish Ceilidh**

We have all encountered different types of group dancing, like the traditional Irish Ceilidh. While advanced dancers may enjoy blending complicated footwork, gentler movements will allow novices to join in the fun, too! The Irish Ceilidh combines various steps that promote bonding, revealing each guest's wish to participate unreservedly. The exuberance and warmth on the dance floor echo the bliss and encouragement the couple receives during their married life's journey.
