## **FAQs**

### **Can Non-Residents Legally Marry in Aruba?**

Yes. Non-residents can absolutely marry in Aruba with proper documentation and Civil Registry approval. The process welcomes international couples and destination wedding celebrations, making it incredibly accessible.

### **How Many Months in Advance Should Couples Start Planning Their Destination Wedding in Aruba?**

Starting conversations with venues and photographers 9 to 12 months before your wedding date keeps options open. Marriage paperwork is submitted 10 weeks before your ceremony. This timeline prevents last-minute scrambling while allowing flexibility if dates shift.

### **What's the Most Cost-Effective Time To Celebrate an Aruba Wedding Event?**

June through September offers the lowest pricing while maintaining beautiful weather. Peak season (November through April) costs 20 to 30 percent more but provides guaranteed optimal conditions and fewer scheduling conflicts with vendors.

### **Do Couples Truly Need a Wedding Planner For Destination Celebrations in Aruba?**

While not technically required, a planner dramatically reduces stress and prevents coordination headaches. Local planners handle vendor communication, timeline management, and problem-solving. Many couples find the peace of mind worth the investment.

### **What Documents Do Individuals Need To Marry in Aruba?**

Couples need certified birth certificates, valid passports, and (if previously married) divorce decrees. English translations aren't required, but certifications must be official. Starting this paperwork gathering three months before your target date allows time for unexpected delays.
