## Why Choose Aruba?

Setting aside the thousands of reasons besides photography, Aruba is the perfect setting for pictures due to its picture-perfect ocean views, soft white sandy beaches, and unique desert landscapes. This island's charm and warm climate make it the perfect backdrop for great wedding photos. Professional, local wedding photographers like G10 Studio have a strong connection to the island and work to build a rapport with the couple, offering some of the warmest memories which will last a lifetime. Generally speaking, something to look out for in a good wedding photographer is that they have a keen eye for light, movement, and detail. With these attributes and Aruba as your backdrop, your wedding pictures will be everything you dreamed of and more. 

### Average Cost of Wedding Photography in Aruba (approximate prices)

The cost of wedding photography Aruba depends on the photographer's experience, the package you select, and the time needed. Here is a general breakdown of costs:

  * **Basic Packages:** These offer a few hours of coverage for $1,000- $1,500, perfect for elopements and smaller weddings.

  * **Standard Packages:** These vary between $2,000 and $3,500, and they are often better packages. They would cover your ceremony, reception, and, occasionally, your pre-ceremony preparation.

  * **Premium Packages:** Package deals incorporating additional services such as engagement sessions, drone photography, and custom albums can cost between $5,000 and $10,000 or more.




Remember to look through all the other services that can be added to the wedding package, such as a second shooter, additional hours, and different edits, in case you have room for some extras in your budget! 

## Factors Affecting Wedding Photography Costs in Aruba

Understanding the factors that affect cost can help you to decide which photographers are both within your budget and align with your vision:

### Experience and Expertise

Professional photographers, such as those at [**G10 Studio**](/g10-studio-aruba-photographer-videographer), are pricey because they have years of experience photographing weddings in Aruba. They know exactly where to shoot on the island, how to capture natural light effects, and how to relax the entire event in order to capture the authentic, unique vibe of your wedding. That being said, an experienced wedding photographer is more than worth all the money spent. Just think about it as an investment because, trust us, you’ll be going back to those photographs time and time again. 

### Coverage Length

It’s pretty straightforward, the more time your photographer spends shooting your wedding, the more you have to pay. Here is a rough guide to the timings photographers offer: 

  * **2-3 Hours:** For intimate ceremonies or elopements.

  * **4-6 Hours:** To photograph the major events and key highlights, such as the ceremony and reception.

  * **Full Day:** This is ideal if you want to capture all the details from morning preparation until late at night with a dance party. It is more of an investment but this will enable you to capture the story of your wedding day from start to finish. 




### Photo Add-Ons and Customizations

Most couples create a customized photo package by selecting an add-on like

  * **Engagement Shoot:** Spending time with your photographer pre-wedding is not just an experience in Aruba, it also allows you to bond with your photographer and get comfortable in front of the camera. Plus, beautiful memories get to made before the big day! 

  * **Drone Photography:** Soar above your wedding with aerial pictures and experience Aruba's stunning beaches and landscape from a unique perspective.

  * **Photo Albums:** These will be an invaluable treasure to cherish for years to come




All of the options above allow you to personalize your experience, allowing your wedding photography to reflect your exact vision.

### What to Expect from Wedding Photography Packages in Aruba

Professional photographers in Aruba vary in the wedding photography packages they offer and their pricing. That being said, the following inclusions are fairly standard practice:

  * **Consultation Session:** The consultation usually includes a discussion with the photographer regarding your vision, types of shots, and general wedding plans.

  * **High-Resolution Images:** Most photographers provide high-resolution digital photos by uploading them to an online gallery or via a USB.

  * **Editing** : Most packages incorporate basic editing like color correction or soft retouching.

  * **Personalized Albums or Prints:** Some packages include custom albums or prints to show off your memories beautifully.




Ask your photographer what is included in the package so you know exactly what you will receive in the basic package and what options are available as add ons. 

### Wedding Photography Aruba Tips

You can make it easy to find the right wedding photography Aruba, with the following steps:

**Identify Your Style:** Research portfolios and find a photographer whose style matches your vision—candid, traditional, or fine art.

**Read Reviews and Testimonials:** Prior customers will be in the best position to inform you about the way a photographer conducts themselves, whether or not they are timely, and whether or not they are able to capture these special moments according to your specifications.

**Ask about the Timeline to Edit:** Know when to expect your photos after editing. Some photographers will provide previews soon after the wedding, while others will wait several weeks to give you your final copies. It is fairly common for the final gallery to take at least a few weeks to be turned over, especially if they cover the entire wedding day. 

**Meet your Photographer:** Whether online or in person, get to know your photographer and ensure that you are comfortable with one another. As an added note, Aruba's tropical climate is pretty warm in most seasons, but there may be a time of year when weddings get busier than others. So, take the time to discuss [specific seasonal details](/blog/the-best-time-of-year-for-a-wedding-in-aruba) with your photographer to ensure that everything will go as smoothly as possible.
