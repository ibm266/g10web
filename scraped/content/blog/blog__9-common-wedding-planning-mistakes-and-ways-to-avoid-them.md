A wedding is an exhilarating time that takes a lot of effort to execute perfectly. Being a groom or bride, you want everything to be bling yet elegant. In the quest to plan everything perfectly, you might make some [**Wedding Planning Mistakes**](/blog/10-steps-to-pull-off-a-sustainable-wedding)**** that cause you a lot of trouble on the wedding day. 

We know you have a lot on your plate. From choosing the venue to how many cousins you will invite, there are countless decisions to be made when preparing for the big day. With so many things to consider, there’s a lot of room for error and mistakes. To help you avoid some of those, we’ve compiled a list of common blunders many couples make during the planning process.

Let’s get into it you might already be making one!

## **List Of Wedding Planning Mistakes And How To Avoid Them**

Of course, you want everything to be just right. Your vendors will do their best to make it amazing, but there are still some common mistakes couples make that even the best team can’t fix. The upside? They’re super easy to avoid if you know what to look out for.

### **Booking Big Stuff Before Setting Your Budget**

You fall in love with a venue. You book it. And then everything else starts breaking the bank.

One of the most common wedding budget pitfalls is locking in major expenses before having a budget chat. Sit down with your partner and figure out what you're both comfortable spending. Once you've got numbers in place, then book that Pinterest-worthy venue or that five-piece band you’ve had your eye on.

Tip: Leave a little room in your budget. Surprises happen.

### **Trying to Play Wedding Planner on the Day Of the Wedding**

You might be a DIY queen, but wedding days aren't the time to field vendor calls or manage timelines. If you didn’t hire a planner, hand off the reins to a trusted friend or family member. That way, you're not stuck troubleshooting cake delivery while everyone's dancing.

Remember, the whole point is avoiding wedding stress, not adding to it. Make extra efforts to savor the day in advance.

### **Running Out of Time Getting Ready**

Between hair, makeup, photos, and that last-minute pep talk with your maid of honor, time flies. Talk to your glam squad in advance and get a clear timeline. Give yourself an extra buffer in case things go sideways (they often do). Being rushed is no fun when you're supposed to be glowing.

### **Not Eating At All**

Yes, the butterflies are real. But skipping breakfast or lunch on your wedding day is a fast track to dizziness or a fainting spell. Keep snacks nearby, even a sandwich or some fruit can make a difference.

Avoid that shaky feeling and keep your energy up from start to finish.

### **Forgetting to Schedule Time With Your Partner**

Strange but true: many couples barely see each other on their actual wedding day. Between getting ready, greeting guests, and endless photos, the day can feel like a blur.

Doing a _first look_ is a great way to spend more time together. Or sneak away for a private moment right after the ceremony; just five minutes alone can help you soak it all in.

### **Skipping the Hair and Makeup Trial**

Yes, it's another to-do on an already-packed list. But trial runs are essential. Without one, you might end up with a look you don’t love or, worse, one that doesn’t hold up all day. Show your artist reference photos and lock in a look you feel confident in.

Avoiding wedding stress starts with knowing you’ll look and feel great on your big day.

### **Assuming the Sound System Will Work Itself Out**

You don’t want your guests straining to hear your vows or, worse, sitting through awkward microphone feedback. If you're hiring a DJ or AV team, make sure they do a sound check before guests arrive.

If your friend is running the setup, they should know the equipment well. A quick test run goes a long way.

### **Skipping the Videographer**

You’ll have photos, sure. But wedding videos hit differently. You’ll hear the laughter, the music, your vows; it’s like reliving the whole thing. If you’re debating whether to hire a videographer, think of it this way: you’re not just documenting an event; you’re saving a memory you can rewatch forever.

And if you're wondering [**how to plan a wedding in Aruba**](/blog/how-to-plan-a-beach-wedding-in-aruba-without-stress) or another destination, video helps you share the experience with folks who couldn’t make it.

### **Forgetting To Enjoy It**

This might be the most important one on our list. It is so easy to get swept up in the chaos of the day and to fixate on everything going according to plan. Don't lose sight of what the day is really about: the celebration of the union between you and your partner! 

Take a moment to step back and focus on what brought you here: the immense love you and your partner have for each other and the excitement you have to start your life together.

### **Bonus: Don’t Forget to Say Thanks**

Your guests showed up for you, some from far away. A heartfelt thank-you speech makes everyone feel part of something special. Make a list ahead of time of people you want to mention, and maybe print a few backup copies of your speech (just in case your original mysteriously vanishes mid-toast).

### **Final Thoughts**

All these wedding planning mistakes are possible and can be avoided successfully by planning smartly. Your wedding is the most auspicious day of your life, and by foolproofing it, you can double the fun. Mistakes are natural, but with smart steps, you can make your wedding a memorable day like you always wanted it to be.
