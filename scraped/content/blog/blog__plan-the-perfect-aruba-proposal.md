Now, let's discuss the proposal planning tips you can consider. The island itself is ideal for making your proposal stand out. ### **1\. Finding the Right Spot**

This is where people make the most mistakes. They see a gorgeous photo online and book it without considering what matters most to their partner. Beach proposals are the obvious choice. [**_Eagle Beach_**](https://www.g10.studio/blog/what-makes-eagle-beach-perfect-for-a-destination-wedding) and Manchebo Beach are well-suited for a photographer who can enhance the proposal. These beaches feel romantic without being artificial. 

Then there's the adventure route. Hooiberg is this mountain on the island. You hike up for about 45 minutes, and a photographer will capture your proposal with a 360-degree view from the top. It's not what everyone thinks of when it comes to a proposal, but that's the point.

**What makes each location different:**

  * **Beach Spots** : Way more straightforward to set up; a photographer can get good angles. It works well for sunset and is less private.

  * **Mountain Views** : More private and feel special; the hike itself is part of the experience; it takes more planning; sunrise light is better than sunset up there.

  * **Unique Locations** : Memorable and different, harder to get vendors there sometimes, less traditional, which some people love.




### **2\. Choosing the Perfect Time**

You might have seen many proposal photos featuring the sunset. That moment feels like a real [**_romantic proposal_**](https://www.g10.studio/blog/proposal-engagement-photography-in-aruba). The light turns golden. It looks incredible in photos. That's genuinely appealing to most people.

On the other hand, sunrise is different. It's quieter. The light is clearer. You won't have a crowd of tourists around. If your partner is comfortable in a calm setting, the sunrise is a perfect time for a proposal and can be especially meaningful.

Here is the Aruba proposal guide to choose the perfect time:

  * Choose the golden hour, as it offers ideal natural light that will make your proposal unforgettable.

  * The perfect time is sunrise, when no one is around, it's just you, your partner, and the photographer.

  * Night is when the restaurants and resorts show their decoration, take advantage of this decoration and plan your proposal at night.




### **3\. Capturing Real Moments**

If your partner likes adventure and wants to try new things, you might prefer to go hiking somewhere scenic, find a fantastic view, and propose with adrenaline pumping through both your systems. That's what would genuinely stick with you. A photographer will guide you to the spot that is perfect for you and your partner so that you can safely spend time together and make your special moment as nobody has before. 

Before planning the Aruba proposal, think about your partner first:

  * **Quiet and private** : Small location, just you two, with a photographer who stays out of the way, somewhere peaceful.

  * **Social and wants to celebrate** : Arrange something with friends, perhaps dinner with people your partner likes, and wants to share it.

  * **Active and loves adventure** : Make it about doing something together, not just standing somewhere pretty, movement, and accomplishment matter.
