# How to Find the Best Locations for Wedding Photography in Aruba?

Dec 17

Written By [Jiten Melwani](/blog?author=62ed087ff4f34a1415eacc32)

Aruba is one of the most stunning destinations in the world for a dream wedding. Known for its turquoise waters, pristine beaches, and vibrant culture, this island offers countless opportunities for breathtaking wedding photography. Whether you envision your photos on a sandy shoreline or against a dramatic natural backdrop, Aruba is the place for you.

When planning [**wedding photography in Aruba**](/photography/wedding-in-aruba), choosing the right locations on the island can make a world of difference. In this blog, we’ll explore how to find the best spots for your wedding photos, ensuring that your memories are as beautiful as the moments themselves.

## Why Location Matters in Wedding Photography

The location you choose for your wedding photos sets the tone for your album and helps narrate your love story. Aruba’s natural beauty provides a variety of picturesque settings, but each couple has unique preferences and themes. Considering these factors ensures that your photos:

  * Reflect your personality and style.

  * Make the most of the natural light and landscape.

  * Capture the magic and romance of the day.




### 1\. Beaches: Aruba’s Most Iconic Wedding Photo Spots

No wedding photography in Aruba is complete without a beach backdrop. Here are some must-visit beaches:

#### Eagle Beach

Known for its soft white sand and iconic Divi Divi trees, Eagle Beach is a favorite for couples. The serene atmosphere and open shoreline make it perfect for romantic, wide-angle shots.

#### Manchebo Beach

If you’re looking for a vibrant backdrop with a tonne of palm trees and a wide open beach, Manchebo Beach is an excellent choice. Sunset photos here are particularly magical. 

#### Boca Catalina Beach

For a more secluded and intimate setting, Boca Catalina Beach offers a shallow, rocky lagoon with crystal-clear waters teaming with fish! It’s ideal for couples seeking a sense of privacy in their photos, complete with a sloping sand bank.

### 2\. Natural Landmarks: Adding Drama to Your Photos

Aruba is home to many unique geological features and natural landmarks that can add drama and intrigue to your wedding photos.

#### Arikok National Park

This park offers rugged landscapes, caves, and dramatic rock formations. The contrast of the rugged terrain with wedding attire creates striking images. In particular, the Quadirikiri light caves are the most popular photography site here. Plus, you can get as a natural fish pedicure in a nearby pond afterwards!

#### The California Lighthouse

Situated on the northern tip of Aruba, this iconic lighthouse provides panoramic views and a touch of history to your photos. It’s especially stunning during golden hour.

#### The Natural Bridge

Although the original bridge collapsed, the Baby Natural Bridge still serves as a fantastic photo spot, offering a blend of ocean waves and unique rock formations. This spot is great for drone photographs as well. 

#### Blackstone Beach Aruba

This is a secluded black pebble stone beach next to the natural bridge, it’s stunning backdrop is a must for all adventure seekers in love!

### 3\. Historical and Cultural Locations

For couples wanting to incorporate a touch of Aruba’s heritage into their wedding photography, consider these cultural spots:

#### Alto Vista Chapel

This small, picturesque chapel is one of Aruba’s oldest buildings. Its vibrant yellow exterior and peaceful surroundings make it a unique photo location.

#### Oranjestad’s Colorful Streets & Houses

The capital city is full of charm, with its pastel-colored buildings and lively atmosphere. A walk through the streets always leads to vibrant and playful photos.

#### The San Nicolas Murals

The history of Aruba’s art scene is depicted all across the walls of San Nicolas, the old town of Aruba!

#### The Butterfly Farm

For a whimsical touch, consider taking photos surrounded by butterflies in this serene sanctuary.

### 4\. Hidden Gems for Unique Photos

If you’re looking to step off the beaten path, Aruba has some hidden gems that can make your wedding photos truly stand out:

#### Bushiribana Gold Mill Ruins

These historic ruins offer a rustic charm and dramatic backdrop for adventurous couples.

#### Conchi (The Natural Pool)

Accessible by a rugged trail, this natural pool, surrounded by volcanic rocks, is a stunning location for adventurous and intimate shots.

### 5\. Consider Lighting and Time of Day

Aruba’s lighting conditions change throughout the day, and understanding them can help you choose the best locations for the time:

  * **Golden Hour:** The hour after sunrise and before sunset provides soft, warm lighting ideal for romantic photos.

  * **Midday:** While the sun is harsher during this time, caves with light tunnels work well during the midday

  * **Sunset:** a must for everyone! Sunsets in Aruba are second-to-none.

  * **Evening:** Nighttime shots under the stars or near well-lit areas can add a dreamy touch to your album.




### 6\. Incorporating Your Wedding Venue

Many couples choose to incorporate their wedding venue into their photography locations. Whether it’s a luxury resort, a private villa, or a beachfront ceremony, your venue can provide:

  * A familiar and convenient backdrop.

  * Opportunities to capture candid moments with your guests.

  * Unique architectural features that add elegance to your photos.




### 7\. Tips for Choosing the Right Locations

  * **Work with your photographer:** A professional photographer familiar with Aruba (such as myself!) can suggest the best spots based on your preferences and wedding theme.

  * **Scout locations in advance:** If possible, visit potential sites before the wedding day to visualize your photos.

  * **Consider logistics:** Ensure the locations are accessible and factor in travel time between spots.

  * **Match your theme:** Whether it’s tropical, rustic, or elegant, choose locations that align with your wedding theme.

### 8\. Making the Most of Aruba’s Weather

Aruba’s weather is typically sunny, but the occasional windy day can impact your photos. To prepare:

  * Choose locations with natural wind barriers.

  * Use wind-friendly attire and accessories.

  * Plan for shade or indoor options during the hottest parts of the day.




### Conclusion

Aruba’s diverse landscapes and cultural richness make it the perfect place for wedding photography. From its iconic beaches to hidden gems, there’s no shortage of stunning locations to capture your special day. 

With thoughtful planning and the guidance of a professional photographer, you can ensure your wedding photos are as unforgettable as your love story.
